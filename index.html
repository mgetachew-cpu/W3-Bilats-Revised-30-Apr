What we are doing and why
By end of June 2026, deliver an updated mapping of W3/bilateral projects to Programmes and Accelerators covering at least 90% of each centre's portfolio by dollar value. This is a formally approved OKR (KR1.7). Two parts: (1) reviewing and signalling on existing 2025 mapped projects, and (2) centres mapping new projects from the updated Anaplan extract.

The completed mapping directly informs three confirmed downstream uses: centre delegate assignments in July, 2026 technical reporting, and 2026 projected benefits. Critical capabilities linkage is the remaining pending item.
The 90% target is a formally committed Key Result under CGIAR's OKR framework:
KR1.7 — 90% of Centers' W3 and bilaterally funded work (by value) included in 2026 technical reports, by Q2 2027.
This sets the ambition: CGIAR's technical reports should reflect the near-totality of what Centers are doing across all funding sources, not just W1/W2. The 2025–30 Portfolio explicitly aims to encompass all of CGIAR's work across all centres and all sources of funding.

In 2025, the 80% threshold was met (285 projects, ~25% of active projects by number). Going to at least 90% is likely to encompass 500–600 projects.

GST confirmed: centres can map any active project within the 90% threshold — not just largest-to-smallest. Mapping above 90% is also permitted, though the delegate assignment threshold stays at 90%.
Mapping is one step in a broader process of portfolio alignment and cohesion-building — not a tick-box exercise. Three layers:
Administrative mapping — the mechanisms through which centres and P/As agree on which W3/bilat project fits where, and knock-on effects (delegate assignment, critical capabilities).
Operational cohesion — W3/bilateral project results included in P/A Technical Reporting (what was achieved). This does not include planned W3/bilat deliverables in P/A PORBs.
Design alignment — common interoperability building blocks (indicators, impact pathways) baked into project design from the start, reducing costs of retrofitting and double reporting. A key objective of the MELIAF project.
Mapping alone is not enough. Inviting mapped project PIs into P/A events and coordination calls is part of what makes mapping meaningful.
Three uses are confirmed:
Centre delegate assignments — July 2026, directly driven by completed mapping (threshold at 90%).
2026 technical reporting — at least 90% of W3/bilat work by value to be included in P/A reports by Q2 2027 (KR1.7).
2026 projected benefits — mapped projects systematically linked to P/A targets via scalable heuristics (e.g. $1m ≈ 5–10k households), applied in Q3 post-mapping.
One item pending:
Critical capabilities (2026) — potential linkage to mapping outcomes, specifics not yet confirmed.
The core process — at least 90% mapping target, centre delegate assignment, technical reporting, and projected benefits integration — is agreed and underway. The GST has confirmed that centres can map any active projects within the threshold (not just largest-first) and that mapping can exceed 90%. The only remaining open item is the critical capabilities linkage.
Mapping rules
Only active projects present in the validated November–December 2025 Anaplan extract are eligible. No proposals are allowed. Active projects started after December 2025 will be handled in the next mapping cycle.

Non-portfolio projects are excluded: these are projects centres have indicated are not applicable for the CSP fee because they fall outside the science and research framework (e.g. infrastructure grants, IFRS depreciation). They are excluded from the mapping process and from the pool used to calculate the 90% value threshold.
Yes — one project can be mapped to a maximum of three Programmes/Accelerators. The total allocation across all P/As for any one project cannot exceed 100%. If two P/As both signal an increase that would push the total above 100%, the centre makes the final call on how to resolve the allocation.
Yes — the GST endorsed "at least 90%", so mapping above 90% is permitted and encouraged for portfolio cohesion and technical reporting purposes. The threshold for calculating centre delegate assignments stays at 90% — anything mapped above that does not change delegate allocation but counts for reporting and cohesion objectives.

Centres should be mindful of transaction costs — mapping more projects means more to report on effectively.
Using the signal module (closes 1 May)
A temporary tool open until 1 May 2026 for capturing P/A signals on existing mapped projects. Log in at: cgiar-program-center-project-mapping.fly.dev using your programme-specific access code and password provided by Elvin Kohen. SSO is not available in this tool.

The full mapping module is a separate, permanent tool at project-mapping.cgiar.org — going live on 18 May for centres. For questions: performanceandresults@cgiar.org. Resources on the P&R Hub W3/Bilat page.
For each project: title (with description on hover), timeline, value, and a reporting indicator showing whether it reported results in the 2025 cycle. Full result detail is available via the Results Dashboard linked in the tool, or the CGIAR CG Results Dashboard. The original mapping rationale (complementarity and strategic benefits) is visible when you click into a project.

Fields sourced from Anaplan (dates, budget, etc.) are read-only — if any data looks wrong, it must be corrected in Anaplan first (contact: Melanie Glover).
Keep as is is the default — if you are happy with the current mapping, no action needed on that project. For changes:

• Increase or decrease percentage — use the slider or type a value. Add a short justification message and save.
• Remove — click remove (sets percentage to 0), add context, save.

Every action is timestamped and creates an audit trail. An Excel export of your full project list with all signals is available for records or offline sharing with colleagues.
Two dimensions, each rated high / medium / low:
Complementarity of results — does the W3/bilat clearly contribute to the objectives and Theory of Change of your P/A? Does its topic fall within your remit?
Efficiencies / strategic benefit — is there geographic focus overlap or strategic expansion potential? Is there partnership overlap or strategic expansion?
These are the same criteria used in the original 2025 mapping and are embedded in the tool's comment function. Use them as the basis for any message you attach to a signal.

Process and decisions
Centre-led. P/As provide a signal — it is input for centres to consider, not a binding decision. In Phase 2 (18 May – 5 June), centres map projects and P/As accept or reject those proposals in Phase 3 (8–19 June). Where mapping is agreed, P/As link the project to a TOC High-Level Output (HLO) or outcome directly in the module. Disagreements are resolved via the built-in chat and if necessary escalated to the GST — though historically most cases have been resolved bilaterally.
Yes. The signal module allows P/As to signal an increase in the mapped percentage for a project. Use the slider and attach a message explaining the rationale. The centre will receive this signal and decide how to act on it.
Once mapping closes at end of June, the PPT will append the updated W3/bilateral project list to your ADAPT PORB — no new PORB is required. The list will also be available through Portfolio Explorer. No further PORB action is needed right now.
Centre delegates to P/As are assigned in July based directly on the completed mapping — which centres have projects mapped to which P/As (up to the 90% threshold) determines delegate positions. This is the most proximate confirmed dependency and the main reason the June deadline matters.

The PPT will share the centre delegate allocation formula so centres can see exactly how mapping informs assignment. Whether delegate assignments will be reviewed annually is not yet confirmed — the Delegate Terms of Reference do not specify a time limit.
